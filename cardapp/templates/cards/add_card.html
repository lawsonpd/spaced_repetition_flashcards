The prompt is what will be shown first when you're learning a card. On a physical flashcard this is what's on the front of the card. It might be a question, but it doesn't have to be.

The answer is what you're trying to memorize or learn. This is what's on the back side of a physical flashcard.

You'll want to format prompts in a way that will make sense when you're learning the cards. Here are a few examples with alternative ways of phrasing prompts & answers:

  • Prompt: What does 'm' represent in 'y = mx + b'?
  • Answer: slope
  • Prompt: In 'y = mx + b', 'b' represents ____
  • Answer: y-intercept
  • Prompt: The capitol of Tennessee
  • Answer: Nashville
  • Prmopt: The capitol of Tennessee is ____
  • Answer: Nashville

It's totally up to you how to write prompts & answers! If it helps, just think of the "prompt" as the front of the card and the "answer" as the back.

The key objective is to make sure that each prompt has a clear and distinct corresponding answer.
